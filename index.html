About Me

The story behind
the product thinking.

One afternoon at Freddy Fresh, I watched a colleague check the workforce app three times in ten minutes — not because she was anxious, but because she genuinely didn't know if her break had been logged correctly. 01
B2B Internal Tool

Workforce & Order Management System

Freddy Fresh GmbH · 2023 · Self-initiated

↓

Context & Research

Observed recurring friction during peak hours in EDPEP (workforce management) and an integrated order management platform. Self-initiated a structured product analysis — nobody asked me to, I simply couldn't ignore it.

Research: Semi-structured interviews with 5 stakeholders — 3 service employees, 1 kitchen staff member, 1 store manager. Focus: daily tool interaction, pain points during peak hours, reliability perception.

Problems Identified

① Break logging uncertainty → frequent manual checks
② Limited shift transparency → coordination friction
③ Cognitive overload during peak hours → attention switching
④ Reactive-only system feedback → no proactive guidance

How might we improve clarity and real-time feedback in internal workforce systems to reduce cognitive load and operational stress during peak service hours?

Core Insight

Key Insight

Internal B2B tools require the same product rigor as consumer apps. Small UX friction compounds dramatically under operational pressure. The system was technically correct — and completely failing its user at the same time.

Proposed Solutions

Real-time break timer with confirmation notification · Weekly hour dashboard · Push notifications for shift updates · Simplified kitchen order queue with urgency indicators

Key Learning

Designing for immediate feedback and error prevention — not just correct functionality — is what separates good internal tools from great ones.

02
Consumer Digital Product

Food Delivery Experience

Lieferando · 2025 · Independent analysis

↓

Context & Research

As a regular Lieferando user, I noticed trust gaps throughout the ordering experience. Interviewed 3–5 friends and flatmates to understand whether these were personal frustrations or systemic issues.

Real User Voice

"I once ordered, the restaurant started preparing my food, but no delivery driver was available. I waited over an hour and a half — the restaurant closed, and I had to raise a complaint to get my money back."
"The app said my food would arrive in 30 minutes, but it actually took over an hour."
How might we give Lieferando users real-time order visibility so they feel in control and confident — even when restaurant drivers handle delivery?

Prioritization

Why Delivery Transparency is #1

Affects every single order · Highest anxiety moment (money spent, waiting, no control) · Directly impacts trust and repeat usage · Solvable with app-side updates without requiring heavy restaurant system integration

Lean Validation (before building)

Mockup test: Show users "Restaurant driver on the way — ETA: 45 min" → Does this reduce anxiety?

A/B test: Text status updates vs no updates → Measure abandonment rate and repeat order rate
